#when-we-lose

When we lose — and when to walk away WIP

Great sellers know when NOT to fight. These four scenarios drain pipeline energy from winnable deals. Qualify early, cut losses fast, stay warm for the re-engage.

Walk or qualify hard SAP inertia

Signal: Customer is S/4HANA wall-to-wall. SAP DRC is already bundled in their BTP licence. IT reports to an SAP Centre of Excellence. The CIO sees eInvoicing as "just another DRC content package."

Why we lose: Integration inertia. DRC is pre-approved in their SAP roadmap and the incremental cost is near-zero because BTP is already paid for. Ripping that out means justifying a new vendor to SAP-aligned IT leadership.

What to do: Qualify in the first call — "Are you running S/4HANA with BTP, and is DRC already in scope?" If yes and they're single-ERP, deprioritise. Compete only if they're multi-ERP (S/4 + Oracle + Dynamics) — that's where SAP DRC falls apart and GLStream wins.

Let them go Price-driven local buyer

Signal: Choosing the cheapest ASP on the FTA list. No GCC expansion plan. Small invoice volume (<10K/month). Procurement is running a reverse auction.

Why we lose: Our value story — Unified API, GLStream, DC/DR, Scale — is overkill for a single-entity UAE SME doing 5K invoices/month. We can't compete on price alone against a lean local ASP.

What to do: Let them go. Stay warm. They'll be back after the first FTA schema change breaks their vendor's integration, or when they expand to KSA and discover their ASP can't follow. Set a 6-month re-engage reminder.

Partner or walk Deloitte relationship lock

Signal: Deloitte is the existing tax advisory + audit partner. The CFO trusts the Deloitte partner personally. InTax is being positioned as "part of our ongoing relationship."

Why we lose: Relationship lock. The CFO isn't buying a platform — they're buying continuity with a trusted advisor. Technical superiority doesn't overcome personal trust built over years of audit cycles.

What to do: Two paths. Partner: offer Clear as the platform under Deloitte's advisory wrapper — "Deloitte advises, Clear runs." Deloitte's InTax uses Pagero globally anyway; they're not allergic to third-party platforms. Walk: if Deloitte won't engage, deprioritise. The re-engage trigger is when the customer discovers InTax's UAE stack and global stack are two different products.

Reframe the pitch Single-country, no GCC expansion

Signal: UAE-only entity. No subsidiaries in KSA, Qatar, or Oman. No expansion plans on the 3-year horizon. The CFO says "we just need UAE compliance."

Why we struggle: Our strongest pitch — Unified API, multi-country, GCC rollout in one integration — doesn't land. The prospect doesn't value what they don't need.

What to do: Don't walk — reframe. Drop the geography pitch entirely. Lead with GLStream (2-week integration, eliminates middleware, recon + VAT built in), DC/DR (3-tier, published RTO/RPO), and Scale (1B invoices/year). These are single-country differentiators that still beat local ASPs on depth. Win on integration speed and operational resilience, not geography.
